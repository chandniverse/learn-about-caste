Caste Studies Resource List

Books

  • "Annihilation of Caste"
    by B.R. Ambedkar (1936)
    This seminal work by the architect of India's constitution provides a critical analysis of the caste system.
  • "Modern South Asia: History, Culture, Political Economy"
    by Sugata Bose & Ayesha Jalal
    This comprehensive work covers the entire spectrum of modern South Asian history, including caste dynamics.
  • "Castes of Mind: Colonialism and the Making of Modern India"
    by Nicholas B. Dirks
    This work examines the role of colonialism in shaping modern understandings of caste.
  • "The Doctor and the Saint: Caste, Race, and Annihilation of Caste"
    by Arundhati Roy
    This book provides a contemporary analysis of Ambedkar's work and the ongoing relevance of caste.

Academic Papers

  • "Caste Exclusion and Health Discrimination in South Asia"
    by various authors (2021)
    This review investigates caste-based inequity in healthcare utilization in South Asia, focusing on those at the bottom of the caste hierarchy.
  • "The intersecting norms of gender and caste in South Asia"
    by Priya Narayan (2021)
    This paper explores the experiences of Dalit women in education and the intersection of gender and caste norms.
  • "A Survey of Caste Among South Asian Americans"
    by Equality Labs
    This study provides insights into how caste dynamics persist in the South Asian diaspora.
  • "Decolonizing Caste and Rethinking Social Inequality in South Asia"
    by Chinnaiah Jangam
    This work challenges postcolonial narratives on caste and argues that caste predates colonialism.

Documentaries

  • "India Untouched: Stories of a People Apart"
    Directed by Stalin K (2007)
    This documentary explores the persistence of untouchability in contemporary India.

Poetry Collections

  • "Touch"
    by Meena Kandasamy
    This collection of poems offers a powerful critique of caste oppression from a Dalit feminist perspective.
